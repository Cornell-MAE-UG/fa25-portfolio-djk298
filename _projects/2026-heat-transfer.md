# Heat Transfer Problem Set 1

## Overview

This project showcases my work analyzing fundamental heat transfer mechanisms such as conduction, convection, and radiation. The assignment included conceptual analysis of heat transfer in spacecraft environments and thermal modeling of a silicon chip cooled by convection.

## Reflection

This assignment helped reinforce the fundamental mechanisms of heat transfer and how their importance changes depending on the physical environment. In the spacecraft problem, radiation dominates in the vacuum of space because there is no fluid medium to support convection and no contact for conduction. However, once the spacecraft reenters Earth’s atmosphere, convective heat transfer becomes the dominant mechanism due to the high-speed flow of air around the vehicle.

The silicon chip problem also demonstrated how conduction within a solid and convection at a boundary interact in real engineering systems. Modeling the chip with insulated sides and back simplified the analysis and highlighted how heat must travel through the material before being removed by the surrounding coolant. These concepts are directly applicable to engineering problems such as electronics cooling, spacecraft thermal protection, and thermal system design.

Finally, the rooftop problem modeled convection and radiation, combining these two heat transfer modes to apply them in a realistic scenario.
