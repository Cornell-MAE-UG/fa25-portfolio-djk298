<p class="section-label">About This Project</p>
<div class="highlight-box">
  As part of MAE 3230, I physically dissected a chainsaw with my group, revealing inner components including the carburetor, flywheel, and piston. Working through the chainsaw methodically, we identified the purpose of each component and how they relate to one another from a fluid mechanics perspective.
  <br><br>
  I took measurements of several key areas to calculate fluid mechanical characteristics such as the Reynolds Number. For our final presentation, I helped set up, calculate, and verify these values — developing an in-depth understanding of carburetors, 2-stroke engines, and Bernoulli's Equation along the way.
</div>
